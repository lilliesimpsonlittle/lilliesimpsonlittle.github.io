Surround Sound for VR Gaming

contact

Here we have my new project, currently just finished and submitted for a unitversity module called Innovation in Sound Practice

It is a wearable piece of technology that measures distance and is worn when in a room with surround sound (currently only working in 5.1). The idea is that the audio moves with you to provide a more immersive gaming experience in VR (and no headphones have to be worn). The whole thing (including the audio) is made procedurally using pure data, an arduino and a couple of unltrasonic distance sensors.

Now here is a nice picture of me wearing it and my (messy) patch in the backgroud.

This is the most advanced patch I have ever made and have incorperated C++ into thie project when compiliing the arduino sketch to allow Pure Data to connect to the distance sensors. The video below explains the process in which I took to make it and also as an example of me wearing the finished project. Please watch and enjoy! (Appologies in advance for the poor sound quality!)
